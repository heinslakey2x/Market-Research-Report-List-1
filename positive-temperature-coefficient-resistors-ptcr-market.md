<p><p>Positive Temperature Coefficient Resistors (PTCR) are devices that exhibit an increase in resistance with rising temperature, providing self-regulating heating and protection in circuits. The market for PTCRs is primarily divided into two types: Polymer PTC and Ceramic PTC. Polymer PTCs are made from conductive polymers, offering flexibility and lightweight benefits, often used in consumer electronics. In contrast, Ceramic PTCs utilize ceramic materials, providing higher thermal stability and durability, making them suitable for industrial applications and power management systems.</p></p>
