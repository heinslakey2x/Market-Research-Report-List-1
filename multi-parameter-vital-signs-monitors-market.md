<p><p>The multi-parameter vital signs monitors market is segmented into three types based on patient acuity levels. High-acuity monitors are designed for intensive care settings, providing advanced features like comprehensive hemodynamic monitoring and advanced alarms. Mid-acuity monitors cater to patients in step-down units or post-operative care, offering essential parameters with moderate sophistication. Low-acuity monitors are suited for general wards or outpatient settings, focusing on basic vital signs such as heart rate and blood pressure, ensuring cost-effective monitoring.</p></p>
