<p><p>The high-pressure coupling market is categorized into two main types: threaded and without threads. Threaded couplings feature mechanical threads that secure connections tightly, providing reliability and pressure resistance. They are commonly used in applications requiring strong seals. On the other hand, couplings without threads utilize alternative fastening methods such as clamps or quick-release mechanisms, allowing for rapid connections and disconnections. This versatility makes them suitable for industries where speed and ease of use are critical, such as manufacturing and fluid transfer.</p></p>
