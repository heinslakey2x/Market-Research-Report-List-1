<p><ul><li>Fusion Devices</li><li>Non-Fusion Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The spinal surgical devices market is segmented into fusion and non-fusion devices. Fusion devices are designed to stabilize the spine by permanently joining adjacent vertebrae, commonly utilizing bone grafts and hardware like rods and screws. Non-fusion devices, on the other hand, aim to stabilize the spine while preserving motion, employing technologies such as dynamic stabilization systems and artificial discs. This distinction allows for tailored treatment options depending on the patient's condition and desired outcomes.</p></p>
