<p><p>The Omega-3 concentrates market includes three primary types: triglycerides, ethyl esters, and phospholipids. Triglycerides are the natural form found in fish oil, offering high bioavailability and better absorption. Ethyl esters are processed forms that can have higher concentrations of Omega-3s, often used in supplements. Phospholipids, commonly found in krill oil, enhance stability and absorption due to their unique structure, providing additional health benefits. Each type caters to varying consumer preferences and health needs in the supplement industry.</p></p>
