<p><p>High-Frequency Oscillatory Ventilation (HFOV) is a mechanical ventilation mode that delivers small tidal volumes at rapid rates, improving gas exchange while minimizing lung injury. The HFOV market is segmented into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive HFOV involves the use of endotracheal tubes to provide ventilation directly to the lungs, typically used in critically ill patients. Non-invasive HFOV utilizes devices like nasal cannulas or masks, allowing for ventilation support without invasive procedures, catering to patients with less severe respiratory distress.</p></p>
