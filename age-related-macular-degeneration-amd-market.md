<p><p>Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) is a leading cause of vision loss in older adults, characterized by the deterioration of the macula. The market for AMD treatments is divided into two main types: Wet AMD and Dry AMD. Wet AMD involves abnormal blood vessel growth, leading to fluid leakage and rapid vision loss, while Dry AMD progresses more slowly and results from retinal cell degeneration. Both types require different management strategies, influencing market dynamics and treatment approaches.</p></p>
